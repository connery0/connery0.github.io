AI Survival

Survive a sandbox with minimal information

The challengeï¼š We were given a premade engine, and had to make an interface for an AI to survive in the game.
The only information available to the bot is it’s own inventory, a changing waypoint and entetiys in it’s vision.
Death is caused by not killing enough “zombies”, running out of health or running out of energy, which drains your health.

The above video will showcase the most important parts of my bots AI.

The bot survives off a dynamic interest system.
It is constantly putting a value on how interesting it thinks multiple goals are.
For example, the interest in heading towards the checkpoint is always positive because it provides 10 points, but it will be seen as a lot more interesting if the AI is already close, or if it’s in an unvisited house.
The interest in exploring the map on the other hand depends on how well prepared the bot is. If the bot has filled up it’s inventory with the nesecary items for survival (weapons and restorative items) it will try to continue mapping out the playing field, to increase it’s knowledge of all available houses.

More information can be found on the projects .readMe file, link bellow

Time Tanks

2 to 4 player arena shooter

The game drops you in a single arena, but props from multiple time periods and random power-ups will change every round into a new challeng for survival.


This is a project made in a team of 2 artists and 3 programmers.
(Pieter Pauwels, Jeroen Wyns, Nikos Van den Broek, Sybren Verheij, Tom Partoens)

My job? My part of this project was mainly focussed on the randomization of the given arena, placing obstacles between rounds and dropping powerups so players have to chase them.
I was also in charge of the power up system, making a system that easily changed the players movement, their projectiles or creating seperate props that last for a fixed time with their own effects.
I also implemented a system to keep track of various actions (riding into walls, scored kills, how often you blow yourself up with friendly firem…) and rank those acording to how interesting they will be, to show to players at the end of the match.

2D engine C++

Creating a game engine from scratch

This project was part of an assignment: We were given the code to display textures and a couple of third party libraries. Our job, was to turn this into a 2d engine and recreate pacman.

The engine supports Multiplayer, the second player can play as ms.pacman and try to get more points or as a ghost and hunt the first player.

  • Other features:
  • Load and parse multiple map files
  • multi-threading enemy ai
  • Mouse control for buttons
  • Exclusive button groupings
  • keyboard and controller support for gameplay
  • keybindings rebindable at runtime
