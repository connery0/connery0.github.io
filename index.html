The Homestead

An rougelike rpg that teaches financial literacy

The Homestead is a game I designed and developed together with Andreas Meletiadis-Megas for Surfstudios over 6 months.
This info and images are shared with permission from the company.
Our job was creating a game that taught the player about financial concepts.

So, we made an action rpg that was fun to play and focussed on earning money and preparing yourself for challenges in an endless dungeon.
Allowing the players to learn about the financial concepts as part of mastering the game.

During this project I acted as the team lead and main designer.
I worked out the core gameplay mechanics and loops, and organized meetings with our employer and our team to discuss progress.
I was responsible for hiring and comunicating with external members such as freelance artists for the project.
And of course I worked on this project as a programmer as well, collaborating on the codebase together with the other team member.
My main responsibility code wise was setting up the fundamental gameplay systems, the inventory and the shop gameplay where you sell your discovered treasures.

City AI Simulation

How to simulate large crowds and make them look natural

A project made in 2 months, the goal was to find a way to simulate a bustling city, one with a large number of inhabitants following their own agendas, while avoiding unnatural behaviour.
The final product easily simulates over 500 agents at 100+FPS.
With every agent adjusting their behaviour to improve the players perception of a lively city and solving collisions and bottleneck areas at runtime.

To allow the player more control and ways to test the implementation, they are also given control over the layout of the town, buildings can be added or removed, and the behaviour of agents can be modified trough events. Forcing the AI to adapt on the spot.

The AI behaviour is based off the Maslovian hierarchy of needs to find individual goals, while their movement is adjusted to adhere closely to human crowd behaviour using Aggregate Crowd Dynamics, this allows the actors to solve collisions of hundreds of agents on the spot. While continuing on their way to their individual goals.

AI Survival

Survive a sandbox with minimal information

The challengeï¼š We were given a premade engine, and had to make an plugin to act as an AI to survive in the given randomly generated world.
The only information available to the bot is it’s own inventory, a changing waypoint and entities in it’s vision.
Death is caused by not killing enough “zombies”, running out of health or running out of energy, which drains your health.

The above video will showcase the most important parts of my bots AI.

The bot survives off a dynamic interest system.
It is constantly putting a value on how interesting it thinks multiple goals are.
For example, the interest in heading towards the checkpoint is always positive because it provides 10 points, but it will be seen as a lot more interesting if the AI is already close, or if it’s in an unvisited house.
The interest in exploring the map on the other hand depends on how well prepared the bot is. If the bot has filled up it’s inventory with the necessary items for survival (weapons and restorative items) it will try to continue mapping out the playing field, to increase it’s knowledge of all available houses.

Time Tanks

2 to 4 player arena shooter

The game drops you in a single arena, but props from multiple time periods and random power-ups will change every round into a new challenge for survival.


This is a project made in a team of 2 artists and 3 programmers.
(Pieter Pauwels, Jeroen Wyns, Nikos Van den Broek, Sybren Verheij, Tom Partoens)

My job? My part of this project was mainly focussed on the randomization of the given arena, placing obstacles between rounds and dropping power-ups so players have to chase them.
I was also in charge of the power-up system, making a system that easily changed the players movement, their projectiles or creating separate props that last for a fixed time with their own effects.
I also implemented a system to keep track of various actions (riding into walls, scored kills, how often you blow yourself up with friendly fire…) and rank those according to how interesting they will be, to show to players at the end of the match.

2D engine C++

Creating a game engine from scratch

This project was part of an assignment: We were given the code to display textures and a couple of third party libraries. Our job, was to turn this into a 2d engine and recreate PAC-MAN.

The engine supports Multiplayer, the second player can play as ms.pacman and try to get more points or as a ghost and hunt the first player.

  • Other features:
  • Load and parse multiple map files
  • Multi-threading enemy ai
  • Mouse control for buttons
  • Exclusive button groupings
  • Keyboard and controller support for gameplay
  • Rebinding keys at runtime
